Color in advertising is not a direct reflection of the environment, but a transformation of it.

This project analyzes how color is translated from natural climates into advertising across polar, tropical, and arid regions. Differences in brightness, saturation, and neutrality reveal how designers adjust environmental color to construct specific visual atmospheres and align with viewer expectations.

These transformations vary by climate, producing distinct palette structures rather than universal solutions. By selectively drawing on environmental cues, advertising amplifies and simplifies color to increase legibility, recognition, and desirability.

An interactive system extends this work by allowing users to generate a personal palette and view the world through a recalibrated color lens.
